Guidelines

Please read, then initial to acknowledge.

This is an informal series of talks given by students, postdocs, and research specialists working in a number of labs on Penn campus who share an interest in the application of X-ray crystallography, electron microscopy, NMR, and other structural techniques to address questions of biological interest. As is to be expected given the science- rather than technique-focus of these labs, talks are not all focused on structure, but cover a wide range of issues in structure, function, and folding of macromolecules.

Membership of this group is open to all permanent members of a participating lab. Rotation students and other short-term lab members are welcome but will not be added to the email list. All dissertation-level graduate students and postdocs must be active speaking participants. Other members of the groups, such as senior undergraduates and research specialists, may give presentations or be non-speaking participants.

A word to speakers new to these talks

Do not be concerned if your project is still at an early stage. We like to hear work in progress and we will try to provide useful feedback. Feel free to discuss your problems and frustrations, as well as your preliminary results. While some may find speaking intimidating, starting early gives useful practice in developing the good presentation skills that are essential for any successful career.

Confidentiality

This is not a public forum. It is a forum for free discussion among colleagues. Information obtained from these talks should be considered a private and privileged communication and should not be shared with anyone outside the group without consent from the speaker and the speaker's PI.

Some simple rules to keep things running smoothly
  • To encourage regular attendance and broad focus, talks do not have formal titles.
  • Talks should be no more than 20 minutes to leave adequate time for questions and discussion.
  • Talks are most often "research in progress," but could also be: a discussion topic (e.g. crystallization methods, refinement programs, etc.); a journal club presentation (reference to be provided to the organizer by the Monday of the week you present, so it can be circulated to members); or a complete story (published or in preparation).
  • Under certain circumstances a 60-minute slot can be requested if needed.
  • If you cannot talk on your appointed day you must switch with someone from the list, and then inform the organizer of the change.
  • A reminder of Structure Talks will be sent out with the names of the scheduled speakers, and of the speakers for the following week.
  • Speakers should arrive early to check that the audiovisual equipment is functioning properly.
